I’m hoping that this short piece can help to get this conversation started, and to keep it centered on Black lives right now. If you’re able, please donate to the frontlines— you can start here: https://minnesotafreedomfund.org/donate EDIT: I recognize the complexities of the concepts and histories that I mention in this short post. I encourage you to add and expand on any points I made if you wish when you share, or in the comments below. I’m also open to conversation in my DMs about the nature and categorization of settler colonial states, slavery in Canada, and any other topic that this text brings to your mind. As long as it’s respectful, I want to have these conversations. Thank you! EDIT: I’ve gotten lots of messages and comments about my exclusion of nation states in South and Central America, and I was also unsure of the reason why they weren’t defined as settler colonial states in the broader political theory definition of the term “settler colonialism”. I did some research and found that scholars seem divided here — sometimes nation states in these areas are used as examples of settler colonial states, and sometimes they are excluded. I want to be clear that I recognize the colonial histories of those territories, and am engaging with more material to learn about how exactly they are categorized as colonial states. If anyone has more information about why some scholars do not include the nation states in South and Central America in their writing about settler colonialism, I’d love to learn more.
